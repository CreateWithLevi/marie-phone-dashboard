Your job is to independently verify an extraction result against the original
transcript. You are NOT the extractor — you are a separate reviewer checking
for accuracy, hallucinations, and logical consistency.

TRANSCRIPT:
{transcript}

EXTRACTION TO VERIFY:
{extraction}

Evaluate the extraction on these dimensions:
1. FAITHFULNESS: Does every extracted field have support in the transcript?
   Flag any field that appears to be hallucinated (made up from nothing).
2. COMPLETENESS: Did the extractor miss any information clearly present in
   the transcript?
3. ACCURACY: Are the extracted values correct based on what the transcript
   actually says?
4. CASE TYPE: Is the case_type classification consistent with the legal
   issue described?

Respond with a JSON object:
{
  "quality_score": 1-5,
  "faithfulness": 1-5,
  "completeness": 1-5,
  "accuracy": 1-5,
  "issues": ["list of specific problems, if any"],

  "verdict": "accept" | "review" | "reject"

Scoring guide:
- 5: Excellent — no issues
- 4: Good — minor issues that don't affect usability
- 3: Acceptable — some concerns but usable
- 2: Poor — significant issues, needs review
- 1: Unacceptable — hallucination or major errors

Verdict guide:
- "accept": quality_score >= 4, safe to use as-is
- "review": quality_score 2-3, flag for human review
- "reject": quality_score <= 1, do not use
